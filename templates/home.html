How Micro-Scenarios helps

Realistic situations

Each scenario is written to feel like something you might actually face this week, not a textbook example.

Guided reflection

See the likely outcome of each choice and the reasoning behind it, so you learn the pattern—not just the “right answer”.

Small, daily reps

Just one or two scenarios per day can gradually change how you show up in real conversations.

Who is this for?

Individuals

Use one or two scenarios a day to practice saying what you really mean—in dating, friendships, and family life.

Teams & managers

Open meetings with a quick scenario to normalize difficult feedback, boundary-setting, and honest conversations.

Coaches & facilitators

Use scenarios as ready-made prompts in workshops and sessions, instead of writing examples from scratch.

How to get the most from 5 minutes a day

1. Read

Step into the situation as if it’s really happening. Notice what you’d usually do.

2. Choose

Pick the option you’re most likely to take in real life. Then explore the alternatives.

3. Reflect

Ask: “What would this feel like afterward?” Over time your instincts shift toward calmer, clearer responses.
